What this tool does

This concrete calculator tells you how much concrete to order for your project. Enter the dimensions of a rectangular slab or footing, or the diameter and height of a round column or tube, and it instantly returns the volume in cubic yards and cubic metres, plus the number of pre-mixed bags required at your chosen bag size. It also adds an optional waste allowance so your estimate accounts for spillage and uneven ground. All calculations run locally in your browser — there's nothing to install and no sign-up required.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate how much concrete I need?

Multiply length × width × thickness to get the volume, then convert to cubic yards (cubic feet ÷ 27) or cubic metres. This tool does it automatically for slabs, footings, columns and tubes, and tells you how many bags to buy.

How many 80 lb bags of concrete are in a cubic yard?

An 80 lb bag yields about 0.6 ft³, so roughly 45 bags fill one cubic yard (27 ft³). A 60 lb bag yields about 0.45 ft³, needing about 60 bags per cubic yard.

Should I order extra concrete?

Yes — order 5–10% extra for spillage and uneven sub-base. Use the waste-allowance setting above so your estimate already includes it.
